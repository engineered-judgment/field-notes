# Start Here

## Field notes, not solutions
You are not reporting what Aspen calculated. You are recording how you thought.

## Weekly notebook entry (10-15 minutes)
Write four short sections each week:

1. **Belief update:** What I believed before this session that I no longer believe, and what changed it.
2. **Assumption + failure condition:** One assumption I made and the condition under which it fails.
3. **Math to physical link:** One place an equation connected to a physical effect in the system.
4. **Skeptical challenge:** The first thing a skeptical engineer would challenge in my analysis.

## Quality bar
A strong entry names a decision, evidence, and a revision trigger.
A weak entry summarizes content without a decision or check.

**Weak (too generic):**
"I understand Aspen better now. I assumed ideal behavior. The math looked right."

**Strong (usable):**
"I no longer assume utility cost is secondary after the energy balance showed reboiler duty dominates near-term risk. I assumed 70% tray efficiency; this fails if fouling drives pressure drop above design. The VLE relation predicted a composition shift that matches the observed temperature pinch. A skeptical engineer would challenge whether this still holds at off-design feed composition."
